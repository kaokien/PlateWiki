This workout runs six rounds. Three minutes each with one minute of rest. Each round has a specific focus. All you need is a heavy bag, hand wraps, gloves, and a round timer.

Round one is your warm-up. Three minutes of single jabs and double jabs. Light power, high volume. Focus on form. Full extension, shoulder to chin, snap the hand back. No power shots this round.

Round two is one-two combinations at about seventy percent power. Jab-cross, move, reset, throw again. Focus on the connection between the two punches. Stay active with footwork between combinations.

Round three is your power round. Full-power combinations. Jab-cross-hook. Jab-cross-hook-cross. Body shots. Sit down on every punch. This round should be exhausting. When you feel the urge to rest... throw one more combination before resetting.

Round four targets the body. Every single punch goes to the body of the bag. Bend your knees to change levels. Never bend at the waist. This round builds the leg endurance needed for effective body work.

Round five is speed. Maximum volume, moderate power. Short combinations thrown at high speed with quick resets. This builds the burst energy you need in the late rounds when most fighters slow down.

Round six is the championship round. Everything you have left. Alternate between thirty-second bursts of all-out combination work and thirty-second periods of active footwork. By the end of this round, your arms should feel heavy and your lungs should burn. This is where mental toughness is built.
