You cannot punch what you cannot reach. And you cannot defend what you cannot move away from. Boxing footwork isn't just about moving around the ring. It's about controlling distance, creating angles, and positioning yourself to hit without being hit.

Every movement starts with the step-drag. The foot closest to where you want to go steps first. The other foot drags to follow, maintaining your stance width. Moving forward, your lead foot steps first. Moving backward, your rear foot leads. The golden rule... never cross your feet.

The pendulum step is your idle state. A rhythmic bounce on the balls of your feet that keeps you light and ready to explode in any direction. The bounce comes from the calves and ankles, not from jumping. Your head should barely move. It's subtle, but it lets you change direction instantly.

The most common mistake is standing flat-footed. It feels comfortable, but it kills your ability to react. Crossing your feet during lateral movement is even worse... one push and you're on the floor. Taking steps that are too large destroys your balance.

The single best footwork tool in boxing? A jump rope. And you don't need a partner for it.
