Boxing gloves are measured in ounces. The standard sizes are twelve, fourteen, and sixteen ounces. For bag work and pad work, twelve or fourteen ounce gloves are common. For sparring, most gyms require sixteen ounce because the extra padding protects both you and your training partner. If you're buying one pair to start... get sixteen ounce. They work for everything.

Velcro closures let you put gloves on by yourself. Lace-up gloves give a tighter, more secure fit but require another person to lace them. Start with velcro.

When choosing a glove, look for firm wrist support, an attached thumb to prevent sprains, and good padding distribution across the knuckles. Cheap gloves use uniform soft foam that compresses flat within weeks.

At the entry level, fifty to eighty dollars, Venum, the Everlast Elite series, and Hayabusa all make reliable training gloves. For a step up, Cleto Reyes, Winning, and Rival make premium gloves used by professionals. But premium gloves are not necessary when you're starting out.

Your starter kit is simple. One solid pair of sixteen-ounce training gloves with velcro closure. A set of one-hundred-and-eighty-inch hand wraps. And a mouthguard. That's it. Everything else can wait.
