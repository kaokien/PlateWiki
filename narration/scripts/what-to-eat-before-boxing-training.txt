When you eat matters more than what you eat. A full meal thirty minutes before training will sit in your stomach and make you sluggish. That same meal two to three hours beforehand gives you clean energy without the heaviness.

Your pre-training meal should fuel both sustained work and explosive bursts. Complex carbohydrates like oatmeal, rice, or sweet potatoes provide steady energy that doesn't spike and crash. Add moderate protein. Chicken breast, eggs, or Greek yogurt. Keep the fat low because it slows digestion. And drink sixteen to twenty ounces of water in the two hours before training. Dehydration kills performance before you throw a single punch.

Training within sixty minutes? Keep it simple. A banana with peanut butter. A small bowl of oatmeal. A piece of toast with jam. These give you fast energy without the digestive load. One tip... avoid protein shakes before training. The liquid can slosh in your stomach during movement.

Stay away from high-fat meals. Burgers, fried food, and pizza sit heavy and compete with your muscles for blood flow. High-fiber meals can cause bloating. And energy drinks give you a spike followed by a crash. If you need a boost, a cup of black coffee thirty to sixty minutes before training is a better option.

Within thirty to sixty minutes after training, eat a meal combining protein and carbohydrates. Protein repairs muscle tissue. Carbohydrates replenish energy. Don't skip this. What you eat after training directly affects how you feel at your next session.
