Your hand has twenty-seven small bones. When you punch a heavy bag, those bones absorb serious force. Hand wraps compress everything together, so your hand acts as a single, solid unit. They also stabilize your wrist, preventing bad angles. 

You'll need one-hundred-and-eighty-inch, semi-elastic cotton wraps. Start by looping the thumb, then wrap around the wrist three times. Keep it firm, but don't cut off your circulation. 

Come up across the back of the hand and wrap across the knuckles three times. Next, go between each finger, returning to the wrist after every pass. Cover the knuckles one more time, use any extra material across the back of the hand, and finish at the wrist. 

Make a fist. It should feel snug and secure. But when you open your hand... you should still be able to spread your fingers comfortably.
