Your stance is the foundation of everything. A perfect cross thrown from a broken stance has absolutely no power. But a mediocre jab thrown from a solid stance? That lands clean. 

If you're right-handed, you'll fight orthodox, with your left foot forward. If you're left-handed, you'll stand southpaw. 

Keep your feet shoulder-width apart, with your rear foot about eighteen inches behind your lead foot, turned out at forty-five degrees. Bend your knees just enough to feel springy, keeping your weight perfectly balanced. Keep those hands up, elbows tucked tight to your ribs, and your chin down. 

A common mistake is standing too square, which exposes your body. Or standing too sideways, which takes away your lead hook. You want a slightly bladed position where you can throw any punch without resetting your feet. 

Test it out. Have someone push you from any angle. If you stumble, your base is too narrow. You should be able to absorb that push... without moving your feet.
