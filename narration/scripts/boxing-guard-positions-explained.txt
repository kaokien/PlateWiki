Your guard is the last thing between your chin and the canvas. A good guard protects you while keeping your hands ready to fire back. 

Let's start with the classic high guard. Both hands sit at cheekbone height, with your elbows tucked tight against your ribs. It's the most forgiving guard and the best place to start. 

Then there's the peek-a-boo, made famous by Mike Tyson. Both gloves sit directly in front of your face, paired with constant, aggressive head movement. It's perfect for pressure fighters getting inside. 

The Philly Shell, perfected by Floyd Mayweather, drops the lead hand low and uses the lead shoulder as a shield. It's incredibly effective, but it requires elite reflexes and is dangerous for beginners. 

There's no single correct guard. Elite fighters switch between them constantly. High guard on the outside, a shell when countering, and peek-a-boo when applying pressure. Your guard should be a choice... not just a default habit.
