The most important punch in boxing isn't about power. It's about control. Legends like Muhammad Ali used it. 

The jab travels in a perfectly straight line... from your chin to the target, and back again. As it extends, your fist rotates palm down. Your shoulder lifts to protect your chin. Then, it snaps back along the exact same path. 

But beginners often drop their other hand, leaving them open. Or they flare their elbow, telegraphing the punch. 

Once you master the basics, you unlock new variations. Like the double jab to break an opponent's rhythm. Or the body jab to open up new targets. It all comes down to consistent, high volume training.
