A parry is a small, sharp deflection of an incoming punch. Unlike blocking, which absorbs force, a parry redirects it. You use your rear hand to tap the opponent's jab across your body, pushing it off target. This clears the center line... and opens a lane for your counter.

The motion is minimal. About four inches of movement with the open palm of your rear hand. Tap the jab to the outside of your lead shoulder. As you parry, load your counter at the same time. Usually a straight cross, right down the middle. And fire it immediately. The opening only lasts a split second.

A bad parry reaches out toward the incoming punch, uses too much motion, or isn't followed by anything offensive. Remember... the parry exists to create a counter opportunity. If you parry and do nothing, you're just playing defense for no reason.

Start by having a partner throw single jabs at half speed. Parry each one and immediately fire a counter cross. Once the timing feels natural, increase the speed. Then progress to parrying double jabs, and eventually... parrying the jab and slipping the follow-up cross.
