Walk into any commercial gym and you'll find a boxing class. Upbeat music, choreographed combinations thrown at the air. It's a solid cardio workout. But it is not boxing.

Real boxing conditioning is built around the actual demands of the sport. Three-minute rounds. Explosive bursts followed by recovery. Sustained guard maintenance. And the ability to throw punches with power... when you're exhausted.

A fighter's conditioning session follows the structure of a fight. Three-minute rounds on the heavy bag or mitts, with sixty-second rest periods. The pace mimics the varying intensity of combat. Bursts of combination work followed by active recovery like footwork and single jabs.

Cardio kickboxing classes use forty-five to sixty seconds of continuous movement without any round structure. The combinations keep your heart rate up, but they don't teach you how to fight. They also build habits that are counterproductive to real boxing... wide punches, dropped guard, squared stance.

You don't need a gym to train like a fighter. Shadow boxing in three-minute rounds is free and effective. A jump rope builds footwork and timing. And the key is structure. If you're training in rounds... you're training real boxing conditioning.
