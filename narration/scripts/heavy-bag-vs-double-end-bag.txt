The heavy bag is a seventy to one-hundred pound bag that hangs from the ceiling. It doesn't move much when hit, which means it absorbs full-power shots. This is where you develop power, practice combinations at full force, and build conditioning. It teaches you what it feels like to hit something solid.

The double-end bag is completely different. It's a small, round bag attached by elastic cords to the floor and ceiling. When you hit it, it snaps back unpredictably... like a moving target. This bag develops timing, accuracy, hand speed, and defensive reflexes. It's the closest thing to a sparring partner that isn't human.

The two bags develop complementary skills. The heavy bag builds power, combination flow, conditioning, and wrist toughness. The double-end bag builds timing, accuracy, rhythm, and keeps your hands up... because it comes back at your face.

If you're setting up a home gym and can only get one, get the heavy bag. It's more versatile. You can work power, technique, conditioning, and body shots all on one piece of equipment. The double-end bag is a specialist tool that works best once you have basic technique down. But if your gym has both... use both.
