The most common mistake in boxing training is doing too much of the same thing. Hitting the heavy bag six days a week doesn't build a well-rounded fighter. It builds overuse injuries and stale technique.

A proper training week balances skill work, conditioning, strength, and recovery. Here's the template. Monday is technique. Shadow boxing, focus mitts, partner drills, slow sparring if available. Tuesday is conditioning. Heavy bag intervals, jump rope, core circuit. Wednesday is strength. Full-body compound lifts. Squats, deadlifts, pull-ups, overhead press. Low volume, high intensity. Thursday is active recovery. Light shadow boxing, stretching, foam rolling. Friday is sparring or technical bag work. Saturday is road work or interval sprints plus a bodyweight circuit. And Sunday... complete rest.

Traditional boxing wisdom says fighters must run long distances. Modern sports science says interval training is more specific to the sport. The truth is somewhere in the middle. A base of aerobic fitness helps you recover between rounds. Sprint intervals develop the explosive energy you use during exchanges. Do both.

Here's something a lot of fighters overlook. Sleep is the most important recovery tool. Seven to nine hours per night. Nutrition is second. Adequate protein, complex carbohydrates, and staying hydrated. Everything else... ice baths, compression gear, supplements... is marginal compared to sleep and food.
